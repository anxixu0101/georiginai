# Dev Log #001 — Rethinking Everything from Scratch

> *Four months of silence. Here's what happened.*

---

## Why the Gap?

I've been quiet for four months — not because nothing was happening, but because *too much* was happening internally. I was rethinking the product from the ground up, and that meant scrapping a lot of what I'd built before.

---

## The Original Idea (and Why I Walked Away from It)

The original concept was simple: a compact desktop device that could monitor **heart rate and respiration contactlessly** using millimeter-wave radar.

No wearables. No skin contact. Just passive sensing, sitting on your desk.

In theory, elegant. In practice, harder than it sounds.

Despite significant work on filtering algorithms, the mmWave radar data remained frustratingly **unstable** — prone to inaccuracies and latency that I couldn't fully engineer away. That forced me to ask an uncomfortable question:

> *Is a device like this something people would actually accept?*

If the core sensor can't be trusted, the product can't be trusted. And I wasn't willing to ship something I didn't believe in.

---

## The Pivot

Rather than push forward with a compromised premise, I made the decision to **change direction entirely**.

I brought my co-founder in to redesign both the PCB and the physical structure from scratch — a complete reset, not a patch.

---

## Where We Are Now

The new PCB is up and running. ✅

I can say this with confidence: what we've landed on is something **genuinely creative and refreshingly simple**. It's a different product than what I started with — but I think it's a better one.
