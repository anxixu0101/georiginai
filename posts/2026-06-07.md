# We Assembled the First Easing-Point and Released a Video

## Easing-Point 1.0 Assembly Complete

At the end of May, we finished assembling the first fully enclosed prototype. Due to budget constraints, we didn’t have an office, so we ended up quickly putting it together in a public restaurant. 

Unlike our initial idea of just building a contactless heart rate monitor, this time I decided to use a pixel pet to visually reflect the user’s fatigue level. The concept is simple: although the mmWave radar has some margin of error when measuring heart rate and breathing, it’s very sensitive at detecting human presence. So when someone sits in front of their computer for too long, the radar tracks their sedentary time — and if it goes on for too long, the pixel pet starts looking tired.

## Current Limitations of Easing-Point

Easing-Point is designed to be plug-and-play via a USB-C connection. To make it more fun, I added a rather silly feature: flipping the lever switch instantly puts it into game mode. So far, I’ve implemented two simple games — a match-3 game and a brick breaker. I believe there’s potential to create more games that can be played using just the lever switch.

![assemble the Easing-Point](/assets/images/13.jpg)

As you can see in the video, the current prototype is still quite rough. It’s made entirely of white 3D-printed parts, and both the enclosure and the lever switch feel crude. Improving the build quality and aesthetics is one of our main focuses moving forward. Our goal is to bring the next version much closer to the product renders shown on the homepage.

## Next Steps

Here’s what we’re planning to work on:
 


### 1. Enhance the Pixel Pet  
I’ve hired a designer to create a dedicated pixel pet for Easing-Point. We also want to make its growth logic more varied and interesting.

### 2. Design more lever-controlled games  
We plan to create additional simple games that can be played using only the lever switch.

### 3. Reduce the overall size  
We’re planning to switch from a 2.4-inch screen to a 2.0-inch screen, as the current size feels a bit bulky.

### 4. Refine the appearance to better match the renderings
We’ll work with a factory to produce a proper enclosure, replacing the current 3D-printed parts with something much more refined.
